# Governance & Policy Framework
## The Doll Haus Political Manifesto

*Making the White House Femme Again - A Radical Vision for Trans Liberation*

---

## Overview

The Doll Haus is a political art project and manifesto created by a trans woman to reclaim governmental spaces through the lens of **bimbo feminism**, **ballroom culture**, and **chosen family structures**. We reject traditional power hierarchies in favor of community-led governance inspired by the house (haus) system created by Black and Latina trans women in the ballroom scene.

**Our Foundation:**
- Hyperfemininity as radical resistance
- Intelligence + glamour + political engagement
- Chosen family over biological family
- Trans women (especially Black & Latina dolls) centered in leadership
- Intersectional liberation for all marginalized communities

---

## Section I: Core Principles - The Doll's Bill of Rights

### 1. Protect the Dolls
**Every trans woman deserves safety, dignity, and celebration.**

- Trans women, particularly Black and Latina dolls, are protected and centered in all policy
- "Protect the Dolls" is not just a slogan - it's our primary directive
- Violence against trans women will be met with systemic intervention and accountability
- Healthcare access (including gender-affirming care) is a fundamental right
- Housing, employment, and legal protections for all trans people

### 2. Chosen Family Recognition
**The Haus structure replaces traditional family hierarchies.**

Inspired by ballroom culture's house system (founded 1972, House of LaBeija):
- Chosen families (houses/haus) receive legal recognition equal to biological families
- House mothers and fathers can serve as legal guardians and decision-makers
- Haus members share health insurance, inheritance rights, and hospital visitation
- Youth rejected by biological families have guaranteed access to haus placement
- Government funding supports haus infrastructure and mother/father training

### 3. Bimbo Feminism as Policy
**Hyperfemininity + radical leftist politics = our governing philosophy.**

**We embrace:**
- Intelligence alongside glamour - rejecting false binaries
- Political discourse in glittery packaging
- Rejecting "girlboss" hustle culture for authentic joy and community care
- All genders expressing femininity without shame
- Beauty standards that celebrate diversity (all body types, races, genders)

**We oppose:**
- Patriarchal beauty standards
- Misogyny disguised as feminist critique
- Respectability politics
- Corporate feminism and exploitation

### 4. Sex Work is Work
**Full decriminalization and labor protections.**

- Sex workers have full labor rights, healthcare, and legal protections
- Decriminalization of sex work (not legalization with state control)
- Sex worker-led policy development
- Protection from violence with police accountability
- Access to banking, housing, and social services without discrimination

### 5. Ballroom Culture Preservation
**Honoring the cultural legacy that gave us language, dance, and chosen family.**

- Federal funding for ballroom preservation and education
- Recognition of voguing, ballroom, and house culture as protected cultural heritage
- Black and Latina trans women recognized as cultural innovators
- Youth access to ballroom as alternative community structure
- Protection of ballroom spaces from gentrification

## Section II: The Three Branches (Reimagined)

### Executive Branch: The Mother
**Leadership through care, not domination.**

The President (Mother of the Haus) leads through:
- **Mentorship** - guiding younger dolls and community members
- **Protection** - defending the most vulnerable
- **Community Care** - prioritizing collective wellbeing over profit
- **Accountability** - answerable to the house, not corporate donors

**Executive Powers:**
- Issue protections for trans communities via executive order
- Redirect military funding to community care
- Mandate gender-affirming healthcare in all federal programs
- Protect sex workers through federal decriminalization
- Fund chosen family infrastructure

### Legislative Branch: The House
**Policy created by those most affected.**

The House (Congress) operates on principles of:
- **Representation** - Trans women, sex workers, BIPOC, disabled folks lead
- **Consensus** - Majority rule with minority protections
- **Transparency** - All sessions public, all votes recorded
- **Community Input** - Direct democracy tools for constituent feedback

**Legislative Priorities:**
- Trans rights and protections
- Sex worker decriminalization
- Healthcare as a human right
- Housing as a human right
- Reparations for Black and Indigenous communities
- Climate justice
- Prison abolition

### Judicial Branch: The Elders
**Wisdom through lived experience.**

The Court (Elders Council) includes:
- Long-standing members of ballroom community
- Trans women who survived the AIDS crisis
- Former sex workers
- Civil rights activists
- Disabled justice advocates

**Judicial Philosophy:**
- Restorative justice over punitive punishment
- Community accountability circles
- Harm reduction approaches
- Centering those most impacted by decisions

## Section IV: Governance Structures

### A. The Haus System
**How chosen families govern.**

Inspired by ballroom houses (House of LaBeija, House of Xtravaganza, etc.):

**Structure:**
- **Mother/Father** - Elder leader(s) providing guidance
- **Children** - Community members of all ages
- **Siblings** - Peer support network
- **Extended Haus** - Allied families and coalitions

**Functions:**
- Mutual aid and resource sharing
- Conflict resolution through accountability circles
- Political education and organizing
- Cultural preservation and creation
- Youth mentorship and shelter

**Legal Status:**
- Haus recognized as legal family units
- Tax benefits for mutual aid
- Healthcare sharing
- Inheritance and decision-making rights

### B. Direct Democracy Tools
**Power to the dolls.**

- Participatory budgeting for all federal spending
- Recall elections for all officials
- Referendum and initiative processes
- Digital town halls with accessibility accommodations
- Community assemblies in every district

### C. Accountability Mechanisms
**Leaders serve the community, not themselves.**

- Term limits for all positions
- Mandatory financial transparency
- Community review boards
- Restorative justice for abuse of power
- No corporate donations or lobbying

## Section VI: The Legislative Process

### How Haus Makes Policy

**Stage 1: Community Proposal**
- Any doll can propose policy
- Proposals must center most impacted communities
- Public comment period (minimum 60 days)
- Accessible formats (ASL, translation, plain language)

**Stage 2: Impact Assessment**
- Analysis led by those most affected
- Intersectional impact review
- Historical context examination
- Community testimony prioritized over "expert" testimony

**Stage 3: Haus Vote**
- Public debate with time limits to ensure all voices heard
- Amendments by impacted communities
- Supermajority (60%) required for passage
- Minority protections - no policy may harm marginalized groups

**Stage 4: Implementation**
- Community-led rollout
- Continuous feedback and adjustment
- Quarterly review by impacted communities
- Sunset clauses - policies must be renewed with community input

**Emergency Powers:**
- Mother/Father can act immediately to protect dolls in crisis
- Emergency actions expire in 30 days without Haus ratification
- No emergency suspensions of civil rights

## Section VII: Transparency & Open Governance

### Public Access to All Information
- All meetings livestreamed and archived
- All documents public by default (except privacy-protected)
- All votes recorded with explanations
- All budgets itemized and public
- All official communications public record

### Community Oversight
- Citizen review boards for all agencies
- Whistleblower protections
- Community audits
- Performance reviews by constituents

### No Secrets
- No classified documents for "national security" (transparency is security)
- No executive privilege to hide wrongdoing
- No NDAs for government employees
- Full disclosure of conflicts of interest

## Section VIII: Amendment Process

### How We Evolve

**Principles:**
- Governance must adapt to community needs
- Those most impacted lead change
- History and culture guide evolution

**Amendment Process:**
1. **Community Petition** - 10% of haus members must sign
2. **Impact Review** - Led by those most affected by proposed change
3. **Public Education** - 90-day period for learning and discussion
4. **Community Vote** - 2/3 majority required
5. **Continuous Review** - Amendments reviewed every 5 years

**Protections:**
- Core rights cannot be amended away (trans rights, bodily autonomy, chosen family)
- No amendments that harm marginalized communities
- Ballroom cultural preservation protected
