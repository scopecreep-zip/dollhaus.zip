Technology for Liberation, Not Surveillance

Building tech that serves trans communities, sex workers, and activists - prioritizing digital security, accessibility, and mutual aid over corporate profit.

Overview

Technology under capitalism serves surveillance, profit, and control - not liberation. Trans people and sex workers need encrypted communications to organize safely, digital tools that don't require government IDs that deadname us, and platforms that don't censor sex work or NSFW queer content. We reject "innovation" that means facial recognition targeting trans women or apps that share data with ICE.

We're investing in technology for the margins: encrypted messaging for activists, telemedicine for gender-affirming care in hostile states, mutual aid apps connecting people to resources without state surveillance, and accessible tech that doesn't exclude disabled folks. Tech should serve liberation, not Silicon Valley billionaires.

Through community-controlled tech cooperatives, open-source tools for organizers, and strict regulations against surveillance capitalism, we're building digital infrastructure that protects trans people, sex workers, and all marginalized communities from state and corporate harm.

Key Initiatives

🔐

Encrypted Communications

End-to-end encrypted messaging for trans activists, sex workers, and organizers. Privacy from state surveillance is non-negotiable for our safety.

💊

Telemedicine for Trans Care

Telehealth platforms for gender-affirming care in hostile states, no government ID required, anonymous if needed. Healthcare without gatekeeping or deadnaming.

🤝

Mutual Aid Apps

Community-built apps connecting people to resources without state surveillance. Food, housing, healthcare coordination through solidarity tech, not corporate platforms.

🚫

Ban Surveillance Tech

Outlaw facial recognition targeting trans women, data sharing with ICE/police, and censorship of sex work content. Technology should never aid state violence.

♿

Accessibility-First Design

All technology must be accessible to disabled folks from the start. Liberation includes everyone or it's not liberation - no ableist "move fast and break things."

🏴

Open-Source Liberation Tools

Community-controlled tech cooperatives building open-source tools for organizing. Workers own the code, not venture capitalists or billionaires.
