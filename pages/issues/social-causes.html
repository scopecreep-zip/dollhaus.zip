Trans Liberation & Social Justice

Centering trans women—especially Black & Latina—in our fight for liberation, alongside sex worker rights, racial justice, and abolition.

Overview

President Barbie believes that liberation requires centering those who face the most violence: trans women, particularly Black and Latina trans women. Our agenda is unapologetically radical: protect the dolls, dismantle carceral systems, and build power from the margins.

We're working to abolish systems of oppression—not reform them. From ensuring trans women's survival to decriminalizing sex work, from defunding police to building mutual aid networks, we're committed to liberation over assimilation. Bimbo feminism teaches us that hyperfemininity, intelligence, and political power are not contradictions—they're weapons against patriarchy.

Through direct action, community defense, reparations, and grassroots organizing, we're building a movement that rejects respectability politics and embraces the fullness of trans joy, sex worker autonomy, and collective liberation.

Key Initiatives

🏳️‍⚧️

Trans Liberation

Protecting trans women's lives through community defense, healthcare access, housing, and legal protections—centering Black and Latina trans women.

✊

Sex Worker Rights

Full decriminalization of sex work, reparations for criminalization, and ensuring sex workers lead policy that affects them.

🔥

Racial Justice & Abolition

Dismantling white supremacy, defunding police, closing prisons, and building community-based accountability systems.

💃

Ballroom Culture Preservation

Protecting and funding ballroom houses, preserving QTPOC cultural heritage, and ensuring haus mothers receive institutional support.

🏠

Chosen Family Recognition

Legal recognition and resources for haus structures, chosen families, and non-traditional kinship networks.

⚡

Antifascist Defense

Organizing against rising fascism, protecting targeted communities, and building solidarity networks for collective safety.
