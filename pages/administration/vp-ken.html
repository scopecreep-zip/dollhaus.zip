About Vice President Ken

Vice President Ken Carson brings decades of experience in allyship, mutual aid organizing, and community care to the second-highest office in the land. His unwavering commitment to supporting President Barbie's vision of trans liberation while using his privilege to amplify marginalized voices has made him an invaluable partner in haus governance.

Born and raised in Malibu, California, Ken developed a deep appreciation for solidarity and the importance of standing beside communities under attack. His journey toward authentic allyship began when he recognized how his privilege could be weaponized for liberation rather than maintaining oppressive systems.

Throughout his career, Vice President Ken has dedicated himself to community defense, mutual aid networks, and protecting trans women—especially Black and Latina trans women who face the most violence. He understands that true allyship means taking direction from those most impacted, redistributing resources, and putting his body on the line when communities face threats.

As Vice President supporting The Mother's executive leadership, Ken coordinates between the Haus Council, The House (legislative branch), and The Elders (judicial branch). He chairs the Community Defense Council, leads mental health and harm reduction initiatives, and ensures resources flow to those who need them most. His role is to amplify, not overshadow - supporting trans women's leadership in all governance decisions.

Role in the Administration

Vice President Ken serves as President Barbie's closest advisor and trusted partner in liberation work. He chairs the Community Defense Council, oversees harm reduction and mental health initiatives, and represents the administration in solidarity networks both domestically and internationally. His portfolio includes building mutual aid infrastructure, supporting sex worker organizing, and ensuring resources reach ballroom and haus communities.

Known for his commitment to authentic allyship, Ken has traveled extensively building relationships with trans-led organizations, sex worker collectives, and abolition movements. His "Community Care" tour centers the voices of those most impacted by state violence, ensuring that the administration's policies are shaped by those on the front lines of struggle rather than imposed from above.

Personal Life and Values

Vice President Ken is married to President Barbie, and their partnership—both personal and political—has been central to the administration's commitment to liberation. Together, they model how power can be shared, how allies can take direction from those most impacted, and how chosen family structures can challenge traditional hierarchies.

Ken practices community care through harm reduction, mutual aid, and direct action. He believes strongly in redistributing resources and has made supporting grassroots organizing—particularly trans-led, sex worker-led, and BIPOC-led movements—a cornerstone of his vice presidency. He understands that wellness under capitalism requires collective resistance, not just individual self-care.
