Join the next generation of trans liberation organizers, bimbo feminists, and community defense workers dedicated to protecting the dolls.

Overview

The Doll House Internship Program offers an unparalleled opportunity to contribute to the work of the Executive Office of the President. Interns gain hands-on experience in public service leadership while supporting the mission of President Barbie's Administration.

Our program brings together talented dolls from across the country to serve their fellow citizens and develop leadership skills that will serve them throughout their careers.

Responsibilities

Interns are integral members of their teams, contributing to meaningful work that impacts dolls nationwide. Typical responsibilities include:

1

Research & Analysis

Conduct policy research, analyze data, and prepare briefing materials on key issues affecting the doll community.

2

Constituent Inquiries

Respond to correspondence and inquiries from dolls across the nation, ensuring every voice is heard.

3

Meetings & Events

Support coordination of meetings, briefings, and official events at The Doll House.

4

Memos & Communications

Draft memos, talking points, and communications materials for senior staff and leadership.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the internship paid?

Yes, Doll House interns receive a competitive stipend to support their participation in the program.

Who is eligible to apply?

The program is open to undergraduate students, graduate students, and recent graduates. All dolls are eligible regardless of their background or field of study.

What is the application process?

Applications require a resume, cover letter, two letters of recommendation, and responses to short essay questions. Selected candidates will be invited for interviews.

Can I receive academic credit?

Many educational institutions grant academic credit for Doll House internships. Please work with your academic advisor to determine eligibility.
